Guests Information

Robert Axtell

Professor of Computational Social Science, George Mason University; Co-Director, Computational Public Policy Lab (Schar School); External Faculty, Santa Fe Institute

Robert Axtell is Professor of Computational Social Science at George Mason University, Co-Director of the Computational Public Policy Lab at the Schar School of Government, and an affiliate of the Department of Economics there. He is also an External Faculty Member at the Santa Fe Institute. His research focuses on agent-based modeling and simulation (ABMS) in economics and finance. He co-authored Growing Artificial Societies: Social Science from the Bottom Up (MIT Press) with Joshua Epstein. His work has appeared in Science, Nature, PNAS, and leading economics journals, and has been featured in outlets such as the Wall Street Journal, Los Angeles Times, and Washington Post. Recently, he has modeled U.S. business firm dynamics at 1:1 scale using >100 million interacting agents, detailed in his forthcoming MIT Press book Dynamics of Firms: Data, Theories, and Models.

Danielle Bassett

J. Peter Skirkanich Professor, University of Pennsylvania; External Professor, Santa Fe Institute

Prof. Bassett holds appointments in Bioengineering, Electrical & Systems Engineering, Physics & Astronomy, Neurology, and Psychiatry at the University of Pennsylvania, and is an External Professor of the Santa Fe Institute. They blend neural and systems engineering to uncover mechanisms of cognition and disease in human brain networks. A Churchill Scholar and NIH Health Sciences Scholar, Bassett earned a Ph.D. in physics from the University of Cambridge and completed postdoctoral work at UC Santa Barbara. Honors include MacArthur Fellow (2014), Sloan Fellow, ONR Young Investigator, NSF CAREER, Lagrange Prize, Erdős–Rényi Prize, and OHBM Young Investigator. They have authored 300+ peer-reviewed publications with 32,000+ citations, supported by NSF, NIH, DoD, and major foundations.

Aylin Caliskan

Assistant Professor, Information School, University of Washington

Aylin Caliskan studies AI ethics, bias in AI, machine learning, and the implications of machine intelligence for privacy and equity. They develop statistically grounded methods to uncover and quantify biases in AI, using ML, NLP, and computer vision to interpret models and societal biases. Their Science paper demonstrated how semantics learned from language corpora encode human-like biases. Caliskan received best paper/talk awards, was named an EECS Rising Star at Stanford, holds a Ph.D. in Computer Science from Drexel and an M.S. in Robotics from the University of Pennsylvania, and was a Postdoctoral Researcher and Fellow at Princeton CITP. In 2021, they became a Nonresident Fellow at the Brookings Institution.

Simon DeDeo

Assistant Professor, Social & Decision Sciences, Carnegie Mellon University; External Professor, Santa Fe Institute

Simon DeDeo leads the Laboratory for Social Minds, conducting empirical and theoretical studies of historical and contemporary phenomena—from cultural evolution over centuries to second-by-second emergence of social hierarchy in non-human animals, from Wikipedia editors to the French Revolution. Formerly affiliated with Indiana University’s Complex Systems and Cognitive Science programs, his work builds mathematical theories to create deep-time accounts of major transitions in political order with an eye toward predicting and understanding our species’ future.

Joshua Epstein

Professor of Epidemiology, School of Global Public Health, New York University; Director, NYU Agent-Based Modeling Laboratory; External Fellow, Santa Fe Institute

Dr. Epstein is Professor of Epidemiology at NYU and directs the NYU Agent-Based Modeling Laboratory, with affiliated appointments at the Courant Institute and NYU Department of Politics. Previously, he was Professor of Emergency Medicine at Johns Hopkins and Director of the Center for Advanced Modeling in the Social, Behavioral, and Health Sciences, and Senior Fellow at the Brookings Institution. A pioneer of agent-based modeling, he holds a Ph.D. from MIT. Honors include the NIH Director’s Pioneer Award and an honorary D.Sc. from Amherst College. His books include Agent_Zero (Princeton, 2014).

James A. Evans

Professor, Department of Sociology, University of Chicago; Director, Knowledge Lab & Computational Social Science Program

James Evans studies the collective system of thinking and knowing—from attention and intuition to agreement, uncertainty, and the topology of understanding—with a focus on innovation and the role of social and technical institutions. He builds observatories for human understanding via crowdsourcing, text/image extraction, and distributed sensors, using machine learning, generative modeling, and social/semantic networks to scale interpretive methods. His research has been supported by NSF, NIH, AFOSR, and philanthropy, and published in Nature, Science, PNAS, AJS, ASR, and more, with broad media coverage. He directs Knowledge Lab, founded the Computational Social Science program at UChicago, and teaches on augmented intelligence, science studies, computational content analysis, and Internet & Society.

Xiaoming Fu

Professor, Institute of Computer Science, University of Göttingen

Xiaoming Fu received his Ph.D. from Tsinghua University and is Professor of Computer Science at the University of Göttingen, Germany. His interests include networked systems and services, cloud and mobile computing, big data, and social networks. He has served on editorial boards (e.g., IEEE TNSE, IEEE TNSM, IEEE Communications Magazine, Computer Communications) and on program committees for SIGCOMM, MobiCom, CoNEXT, INFOCOM, ICNP, and COSN. He is a Fellow of IET and Academia Europaea, an IEEE Senior Member, and an IEEE Distinguished Lecturer.

Xin Gao

Ph.D. Candidate, Department of Sociology, Tsinghua University

Xin Gao is a Ph.D. candidate in Sociology at Tsinghua University. Her research interests include social network studies and organizational behavior.

Xiaobin He

Associate Professor, Department of Sociology, Tsinghua University

Xiaobin He received his M.S. in Statistics and Ph.D. in Sociology from Stanford University (2006, 2010). His research spans organizational and economic sociology, entrepreneurship and innovation, and quantitative methods. He has published 60+ papers in journals such as Computers in Human Behavior, Entrepreneurship Theory and Practice, Social Science Research, and top Chinese journals, and has led or participated in over 10 state-funded projects. Recent topics include market transition and social transformation in China, gig-economy social security, and the social foundations of innovation.

Bo Huang

Professor, Computational Social Science Laboratory, Faculty of Social Science, The Chinese University of Hong Kong

Bo Huang is Professor at CUHK, CJ Scholar Chair Professor at Southwest Jiaotong University, and Associate Director of the Institute of Space and Earth Information Science. He received the MoE Higher Education Outstanding Scientific Research Output Award (2020, Second Class, Natural Sciences) for work on spatiotemporal multi-scale data fusion. His interests include spatio-temporal analytics, GIS for sustainable urban/transportation/land planning, unified image fusion for sustainable environments, spatial statistics for land-use change, and multi-objective optimization for spatial planning considering vulnerability and resilience.

Jianmin Jia

Presidential Chair Professor, Shenzhen Finance Institute, The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen

Prof. Jia previously served as Professor and Chair of Marketing at CUHK, Chang Jiang Chair Professor, and Dean of the School of Economics and Management at Southwest Jiaotong University. He has held roles with the National MBA Education Supervisory Committee, NSFC’s Management Sciences Department, and as Associate Editor of Operations Research. Recipient of the 2019 Fudan Outstanding Contribution Award of Management, his research covers big-data behavioral research, social networks, and decision making under risk, with publications in Nature, Nature Communications, Management Science, Marketing Science, and more.

Jar-Der Luo

Professor, School of Social Sciences & School of Public Policy and Management, Tsinghua University; Chair, Tsinghua Social Network Research Center

Jar-Der Luo is Professor of Sociology at Tsinghua University, President of the Chinese Network for Social Network Studies, and Chair of the Tsinghua Social Network Research Center. He earned his Ph.D. in Sociology from SUNY Stony Brook. His research spans social capital, trust, big-data social network analysis, self-organization, and indigenous Chinese management (e.g., guanxi and guanxi circles). He also leads community revitalization initiatives integrating public-private partnerships and sustainable development.

Jan Nagler

Professor, Frankfurt School of Finance & Management

Trained in physics (Ph.D. on dynamical systems/chaotic astrophysics), Jan Nagler held positions at Boston University, the Max Planck Institute for Dynamics and Self-Organization, and ETH Zurich (Group Leader, ETH Risk Center). Since 2016 he has served as Vice Chair of the Physics of Socio-economic Systems Division of the German Physical Society. His research addresses networked stochastic systems at the interface of physics, biology, and socio-economics (game theory, phase transitions, ergodicity breaking, risk and survival). In 2018 he founded the Deep Dynamics Group in Frankfurt. His work appears in Nature, Science, and Nature Physics; he also hosts podcasts (since 2020).

Oshani Seneviratne

Director of Health Data Research, Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute

Dr. Seneviratne earned her Ph.D. in Computer Science at MIT under Sir Tim Berners-Lee. Her research spans data integration in knowledge graphs, AI, accountable and decentralized systems (web and blockchain). She has 50+ publications and received the Yahoo! Key Scientific Challenge Award. She co-founded the AIChain workshop (IEEE Blockchain), Personal Health Knowledge Graph workshop (KGC), and co-organized the AAAI Symposium on AI for Social Good. She has served on organizing committees for ISWC and Web Science, and as Program Chair (Web Science 2022), and is a co-editor and reviewer for multiple journals.

Gareth Tyson

Senior Lecturer, Queen Mary University of London; Fellow, The Alan Turing Institute

Gareth Tyson works in Internet Data Science, focusing on networking and web measurements, with publications in SIGMETRICS, WWW, IMC, ICWSM, and more. His research has been covered by MIT Tech Review, Washington Post, BBC, The Times, Wired, Science Daily, Ars Technica, The Independent, Business Insider, and The Register.

Feiyue Wang

Professor & Director, State Key Laboratory of Management and Control for Complex Systems, Institute of Automation, Chinese Academy of Sciences

Prof. Wang’s research and teaching cover modeling, analysis, and control mechanisms of complex systems, Linguistic Dynamic Systems (LDS), intelligent control systems, parallel intelligence, social energy and social computing, and knowledge automation.

Xinyue Ye

Harold L. Adams Endowed Professor, Department of Landscape Architecture & Urban Planning, Texas A&M University; Director, Urban Data Science Lab

Dr. Ye’s research in geospatial AI and urban informatics models the space-time dynamics of socioeconomic inequality and human activity, with applications in disaster response, economic development, public health, and transportation. He has received faculty research awards in both computing and geography, with funding from NSF, NIJ, the Department of Commerce, and the Department of Energy.
