3.2. Evolution

3.2.0.9. {microEvol_kdd08} Leskovec, Jure, et al. “Microscopic evolution of social networks.” Proceedings of the 14th ACM SIGKDD international conference on Knowledge discovery and data mining. ACM, 2008.

  • Motivated by these observations in paper, they present a complete network evolution model:
    • Nodes arrive using the node arrival function N(·).
    • Node u arrives and samples its lifetime a from the exponential distribution p(a) = λ exp(−λa).
    • Node u adds the first edge to node v with probability proportional to its degree.
    • A node u with degree d samples a time gap δ from the distribution p(δ|d; α, β) = (1/Z)δ −α exp(−βdδ) and goes to sleep for δ time steps.
    • When a node wakes up, if its lifetime has not expired yet, it creates a two-hop edge using the random-random triangle-closing model.
    • If a node’s lifetime has expired, then it stops adding edges; otherwise it repeats from step 4.
  • Figure 9 shows the different trend of two parameters.
  • The definition of edge attachment by degree (formula 1) and edge by the age of the node (formula 2).
